## Summary Takeaway

ResiliMass Wall (6/4/6) pairs a stabilized rammed earth exterior shell, mid-wall continuous insulation, and a vapor-open hempcrete infill to chase thermal mass, a tight envelope, and resilient interior comfort. The heavy rammed earth is the only load-bearing element; the interior hempcrete is non-structural and is attached via anchors that are embedded during the exterior pour (they do not carry gravity loads). This is a pilot-ready configuration best suited for owner-builders, custom homes, or engineered prototypes where both mass performance and hygroscopic breathability are priorities.

## Assembly Description

The system layers (from outside in) are:

- 6" stabilized rammed earth exterior wall (structural, moisture-tolerant, high thermal mass)
- 4" continuous insulation layer (mineral wool or wood fiber)
- 6" interior hempcrete infill (non-structural, vapor-open finish)

Embedded anchors cast into the rammed earth capture the insulation layer and hempcrete, but only provide mechanical connectionâ€”they are not sized for gravity loads. This keeps the rammed earth as the sole load-bearing element while allowing the interior mass to act as a passive climate buffer.

## Constructability Notes

- Requires coordinated sequencing: exterior rammed earth pour must include anchors, insulation is mechanically anchored afterward, then the hempcrete is packed in from the interior.
- Skilled rammed earth crew plus experienced builders familiar with hempcrete mixes are essential to keep schedules tight.
- Anchors must be inspected before insulation and hempcrete placement to ensure alignment; they are not structural but they anchor the cladding system.

## Moisture & Durability Considerations

- The exterior rammed earth is moisture-tolerant and weather-resistant when finished properly; a breathable exterior seal (lime wash or mineral coating) helps deflect driving rain.
- The continuous insulation keeps the thermal mass warm while allowing the hempcrete to dry inward; the hempcrete itself dries slowly but remains vapor-open.
- Interior finishes should respect the breathability to avoid trapping moisture between layers.

## When This System Makes Sense

- Pilot projects, owner-builders, and custom residences seeking a mass wall with breathable finishes.
- Sites where daylight-driven thermal mass can be leveraged to store heat from passive solar gains.
- Applications where robustness (fire, pests) and indoor comfort outweigh the premium labor investment.
